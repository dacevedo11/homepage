Research

I have come to realize that games and interactive media have so many more applications beyond entertainment. My diverse research interests lie in exploring the various roles these technologies can play in user experience, cultural and social change, and health.

I have always been fascinated by narratives and the art of storytelling, with a specific interest in exploring diverse cultures through their folklore and mythology. I hope to explore the different ways in which narrative can create meaningful and interactive experiences. I believe narrative is a great way to explore and promote diverse cultural perspectives. While many games often take inspiration from Greek or Norse mythology, like God of War or Hades, I instead want to bring attention to underrepresented cultures that are commonly overlooked and challenge biases within mainstream media. My involvement in researching resilience as an undergraduate research assistant at the Game User Interaction and Intelligence Lab has sparked a personal interest in leveraging games to address mental health challenges, particularly PTSD among veterans. Given my own background as a disabled veteran, this is an area I am keen to explore. Inspired by previous studies exploring the therapeutic benefits of games for dealing with conditions like PTSD, I aspire to contribute to this field of research and pave the way for fellow veterans seeking opportunities within the gaming industry.
